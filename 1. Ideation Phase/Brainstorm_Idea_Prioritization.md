# Brainstorm Idea Prioritization

## Executive Summary
The goal of the Nutrition Assistant is to simplify diet tracking while providing actionable insights. We brainstormed various features to address common user pain points in existing diet apps. Below is the prioritization of these ideas based on their impact and feasibility.

---

## 1. High Priority (Must Haves for MVP)

These features form the core value proposition of our application and must be included in the initial launch.

*   **Comprehensive Meal Tracking System**: A robust system to log breakfast, lunch, dinner, and snacks.
*   **Custom Goal Setting**: Allow users to set specific goals (weight loss, maintenance, muscle gain) and calculate required daily macros (Protein, Carbs, Fats) and calories.
*   **Personalized Profile**: Collection of user metrics including age, weight, height, gender, activity level, and dietary restrictions to accurately calculate BMR and TDEE.
*   **Dynamic Dashboard**: A visual representation of daily progress, showing remaining calories and macros in real-time as meals are logged.

## 2. Medium Priority (Should Haves for V2)

Features that significantly enhance user experience but are not critical for the base functionality.

*   **Tailored Recipe Recommendations**: Suggest recipes based on the user's remaining macros for the day to eliminate guesswork.
*   **Grocery List Auto-generation**: Generate a weekly shopping list based on selected recipes and meal plans.
*   **Water Tracking**: A simple interface to log daily water intake, essential for overall nutrition and health.
*   **Progress Charts**: Historical data visualization showing weight changes over time against caloric intake.

## 3. Low Priority (Could Haves / Future Roadmap)

Innovative or supplementary features that will differentiate the app in the long term.

*   **AI Meal Recognition**: Use the device camera to take a picture of a meal and use AI to estimate calories and macros automatically.
*   **Dietitian Consultations**: A portal to connect with certified nutritionists for personalized advice and accountability.
*   **Social Challenges & Leaderboards**: Community features allowing users to create weight-loss or consistency challenges with friends.
*   **Wearable Integration**: Syncing with Apple Health, Google Fit, and Garmin to automatically pull in activity data and adjust daily caloric needs.

---

## Prioritization Matrix Justification
The prioritization is driven by the need to first establish a reliable baseline of data (High Priority) before introducing intelligent layers on top of that data (Medium Priority) and finally moving into community and advanced tech features (Low Priority).
